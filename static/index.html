🔑 Feature Importances

What Drives Congestion?

The Random Forest model identifies historical load as the dominant predictor of congestion, followed by infrastructure age. Together, these two features account for over 85% of the model's predictive power.

The interaction between high load and old infrastructure creates a compounding risk — aging lines under heavy demand are the most likely to fail.

💡 The model uses 9 features to predict congestion scores between 0.0 (clear) and 1.0 (critical overload).

Resilience

Self-Healing Mechanism

When a transmission line fails, the system automatically detects the failure, verifies connectivity via DFS, and reroutes through alternate paths.

Healing Cycle Steps

1

🔍 Detect At-Risk Edge

ML predictor identifies the most congested transmission line based on current conditions.

2

⚡ Simulate Line Failure

The highest-congestion edge is removed from the graph to simulate a real failure.

3

🔗 DFS Connectivity Check

Depth-First Search verifies the network remains connected after the failure.

4

🔄 A* Reroute

The A* algorithm calculates an alternate optimal path avoiding the failed line.
